Inquire Biology

An intelligent textbook that answers students’ questions, engages their interest, and improves their understanding.

Inquire was a research project combining a question-answering AI with an AP Biology textbook. In a 2012 evaluation, students using Inquire received higher test scores than those without Inquire.

I was product manager for a team of ten developers, researchers, and educators, as well as designer and developer of the iPad app. I later helped create an award-winning video and the project website.

  • Inquire creates a Wikipedia-style glossary page for every concept in the book. Links and related figures help students make connections between concepts.
  • Asking a question. Inquire suggests questions as you type, illustrating the types of questions it can answer and the best ways to phrase them.
  • An answer comparing two concepts. Key differences are shown first (e.g., functions); similar differences within a section are aligned (e.g., parts).

Perls

A pervasive learning platform that uses your interests and your context to suggest the best lesson to learn right now.

Perls is an ongoing research project to create a context-aware education platform. Based on a user’s learning goals, location, schedule, patterns of use, and other data, Perls creates a path through a rich web of content and then tailors its recommendations to the user’s current situation.

I am the design lead and iPhone developer for Perls.

  • Swipe through Perls’ top suggestions as cards. Here & Now recommends lessons that are of immediate benefit (e.g., nearby opportunities or upcoming deadlines) or especially well-suited to your current activity (such as podcasts for your commute or a how-to manual at the office photocopier).
  • If you’re killing time in the checkout line, the Quick Pick list will find something you can learn in a few minutes.
  • If you want something different, Surprise Me steps outside the things you’re studying to find other content you may enjoy.
  • Learning isn’t always fun; sometimes you just have to get it done. To Do has your top priorities and assignments all in one place.
  • Over time, Perls will learn more about your interests and study patterns. The occasional “action card” may suggest new goals (“Interested in Augmented Reality?”) or better habits (“You’d better pick up the pace!”).
  • Your path through a topic is defined by your goal, level of interest, and current expertise. Perls boils this down to four simple options: Explore, Study, Master, and Stay Sharp. Once you make a choice, Perls can filter and sequence the content to suit your path.
